## Overview

Store partners are local businesses that participate in GreenGuard's rewards marketplace by offering discount coupons that citizens can redeem using earned points. This role creates a mutually beneficial relationship: citizens receive tangible rewards for community engagement, while stores gain visibility and attract motivated customers.

<Note>
Store partners benefit from a pre-qualified customer base that has demonstrated community engagement and earned points specifically to spend at partner businesses.
</Note>

## Store Profile Setup

### Store Information

Each store partner maintains a profile containing:

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Store Name** | Business name displayed in the marketplace |
| **District** | Primary location for local customer targeting |
| **Owner/Manager** | Associated user account managing the store |
| **Contact Information** | Phone and email from the user account |
| **Operational Status** | Active store indicator |

### District-Based Visibility

Stores are associated with specific municipal districts:

- **Local Discovery**: Citizens can filter coupons by nearby stores in their district
- **Community Connection**: Build relationships with local residents reporting incidents
- **Targeted Marketing**: Offers reach customers most likely to visit your location
- **Geographic Analytics**: Track which districts generate the most redemptions

## Creating Coupons

### Coupon Design Process

<Steps>
  <Step title="Define the Offer">
    Decide on the discount value, product/service included, and any restrictions or limitations.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set Point Value">
    Determine how many points citizens must spend to redeem the coupon. Consider the offer value and desired redemption volume.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose Category">
    Select the appropriate category: Food & Restaurants, Retail & Clothing, Groceries, or Electronics.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Write Description">
    Craft a compelling description that clearly explains the offer value and any terms or conditions.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set Availability">
    Publish the coupon to make it visible in the rewards marketplace.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Coupon Attributes

Store partners configure the following fields when creating coupons:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Coupon Name" icon="heading">
    **Format**: Short, descriptive title (up to 50 characters)
    
    **Examples**:
    - "20% Off Any Pizza"
    - "$10 Off $50 Purchase"
    - "Free Dessert with Entree"
    - "Buy One Get One 50% Off"
    
    **Best Practices**: Make the value proposition immediately clear to attract citizen attention.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Description" icon="file-lines">
    **Format**: Detailed explanation (up to 50 characters)
    
    **Include**:
    - Specific items or services covered
    - Any exclusions or restrictions
    - Validity period or usage limitations
    - Special terms or minimum purchase requirements
    
    **Example**: "Valid on all menu items. Dine-in only. One per customer. Excludes alcohol."
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Category" icon="layer-group">
    **Available Categories**:
    - **Food & Restaurants**: Dining, beverages, catering
    - **Retail & Clothing**: Apparel, accessories, fashion
    - **Groceries**: Supermarkets, produce, household items
    - **Electronics**: Appliances, gadgets, devices
    
    Citizens browse by category, so accurate classification improves discoverability.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Points Required" icon="coins">
    **Pricing Strategy**: Balance between attractiveness to citizens and business margins.
    
    **Typical Ranges**:
    - 50-100 points: Small discounts, low-value items
    - 100-250 points: Medium discounts, moderate purchases
    - 250-500 points: Large discounts, premium offers
    - 500+ points: Major discounts, high-value items
    
    Consider that citizens earn points by reporting incidents, so pricing should reflect community effort.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Coupon Code" icon="barcode">
    **Format**: Unique alphanumeric identifier (up to 20 characters)
    
    **Purpose**:
    - Internal tracking and verification
    - Reference for citizen inquiries
    - Integration with POS systems
    
    **Example**: `GGCPN2026Q1001`, `SPRING20`, `WELCOME10`
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Active Status" icon="toggle-on">
    **Control Availability**: Enable or disable coupon visibility in the marketplace.
    
    **Use Cases**:
    - Temporarily pause during high redemption volume
    - Seasonal offers (activate/deactivate as needed)
    - Inventory management (disable when out of stock)
    - A/B testing different offers
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Managing Coupons

### Coupon Lifecycle

Store partners control coupon availability and inventory:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Active Coupons">
    **Visible in Marketplace**: Citizens can browse and redeem these coupons.
    
    **Management Actions**:
    - Monitor redemption rates
    - Adjust points required if needed
    - Track which coupons generate most interest
    - Temporarily deactivate during inventory issues
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Inactive Coupons">
    **Hidden from Marketplace**: Not available for citizen redemption.
    
    **Common Reasons**:
    - Out of stock or inventory limitations
    - Seasonal offer outside valid period
    - Testing or preparing new campaigns
    - Excessive redemption volume
    
    Inactive coupons can be reactivated at any time without creating new entries.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Historical Coupons">
    **Past Campaigns**: Expired or discontinued offers retained for analytics.
    
    **Value**:
    - Review redemption performance
    - Identify successful offer structures
    - Plan future campaigns based on data
    - Calculate ROI and customer acquisition costs
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Redemption Process

### How Citizens Redeem Coupons

Understanding the citizen redemption workflow helps stores prepare for in-store validation:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Citizen Selects Coupon">
    Customer browses the marketplace and chooses your offer, spending their earned points.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Coupon Generated">
    System creates a unique redemption record with a QR verification code and coupon code.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Customer Visits Store">
    Citizen presents their phone showing the coupon QR code at your location.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Store Scans QR Code">
    Your staff uses the verification system to scan and validate the code.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Redemption Confirmed">
    System marks the coupon as redeemed, preventing duplicate usage, and finalizes the transaction.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### QR Code Verification

Each redeemed coupon includes a unique QR verification code:

- **Security**: Prevents fraud and duplicate redemptions
- **Tracking**: Links specific redemptions to citizens and coupons
- **Analytics**: Provides data on which offers drive foot traffic
- **Validation**: Confirms coupon hasn't expired or been used previously

<Warning>
Always scan the QR verification code before providing the discount. The code ensures the coupon is valid and hasn't been previously redeemed.
</Warning>

### Coupon States

Redeemed coupons progress through three states:

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Active (AC)" icon="circle-check" color="#10b981">
    Citizen has redeemed the coupon with points but hasn't used it at the store yet. Ready for scanning.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Redeemed (CA)" icon="check-double" color="#6366f1">
    Store has scanned the QR code and applied the discount. Transaction complete.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Expired (VE)" icon="circle-xmark" color="#ef4444">
    Coupon validity period has passed without store redemption. Cannot be used.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Analytics and Insights

### Tracking Performance

Store partners can access valuable data about coupon performance:

| Metric | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Total Redemptions** | Number of coupons redeemed at your store |
| **Active Coupons** | Citizens holding unredeemed coupons for your offers |
| **Popular Offers** | Which coupons generate the most redemptions |
| **Peak Redemption Times** | When citizens typically visit to use coupons |
| **District Distribution** | Which neighborhoods drive the most traffic |
| **Conversion Rate** | Ratio of coupon views to actual redemptions |
| **Average Points Spent** | Typical point investment per citizen transaction |

### Data-Driven Optimization

Use analytics to improve coupon strategy:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Identify Top Performers" icon="trophy">
    Analyze which coupon structures (discount percentage, point costs, categories) generate the most redemptions. Replicate successful patterns.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Optimize Point Pricing" icon="dollar-sign">
    If redemption rates are too low, the points required might be too high. If rates are excessive, you may be underpricing the offer.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Test Offer Variations" icon="flask">
    Create similar coupons with different values or restrictions to determine what resonates with citizens.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Seasonal Timing" icon="calendar-days">
    Review historical data to identify optimal times for specific product categories and plan campaigns accordingly.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Customer Retention" icon="rotate">
    Track whether citizens who redeem coupons become repeat customers or make additional purchases beyond the discount.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Best Practices

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Create Compelling Offers" icon="sparkles">
    Make discounts genuinely valuable to motivate citizens to spend their hard-earned points at your store rather than competitors.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Price Competitively" icon="scale-balanced">
    Balance point costs with discount value. Consider that citizens earn roughly 10-20 points per incident report, so a 100-point coupon represents significant effort.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Keep Inventory Current" icon="boxes-packing">
    Deactivate coupons immediately if you run out of stock. Nothing frustrates customers more than discovering an offer isn't actually available.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Train Staff" icon="chalkboard-user">
    Ensure all employees understand the QR verification process and can smoothly handle coupon redemptions without confusion.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Provide Excellent Service" icon="handshake">
    Remember that coupon redeemers are community-minded citizens who've earned their rewards. Treat them exceptionally to build loyalty.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Monitor Redemption Rates" icon="chart-mixed">
    Regularly review analytics to understand which offers work and adjust your strategy accordingly.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Refresh Offers Regularly" icon="arrows-rotate">
    Update your coupon portfolio periodically to maintain citizen interest and showcase seasonal products or services.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Clear Terms" icon="list-check">
    Explicitly state any restrictions, minimum purchases, or exclusions in the description to avoid customer disappointment.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
